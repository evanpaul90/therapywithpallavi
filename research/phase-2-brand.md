## The one sentence

> **Therapy for ambitious Indian professionals who've outgrown their coping strategies — with a therapist who's lived in boardrooms and still reads the research.**

That's the anchor. Every word on the site orbits it.

---

## Why this positioning, and what we're rejecting

### What the current site says
> "I am a Counsellor & Psychotherapist… confidential and safe space… trauma-informed and inclusive…"

This is what *every* therapist website says. There are ~200 practising counsellors in Bangalore alone. On this framing, Pallavi is one of them.

### What her creds actually say
- **MA Clinical Psychology** — foundational, necessary-not-sufficient
- **CBT, NLP, QPR, IFS** — evidence-based modalities, IFS is premium for trauma
- **PGDM (management)** — *almost no Indian therapist has this*
- **8+ years practice · 200+ clients guided**
- **Bangalore-based, online globally**

The PGDM is the asymmetric differentiator. It means she understands performance reviews, political org dynamics, the specific ambition-and-shame cocktail of high-performing professionals, the way Indian families convert achievement into love-withheld, the way partners feel abandoned by a Sunday afternoon spent on email. She can *name* the world her clients live in. Most therapists can't.

### The positioning rejection
**We are not** positioning her as a general-practice therapist who also happens to be good with professionals. We are positioning her as *the therapist who speaks corporate-and-inner-life fluently*, whose practice happens to extend to broader concerns. This is the direction of causality that matters.

### Who this excludes (deliberately)
- Crisis-only callers who want one session to stop the bleeding
- Clients shopping purely on price
- Seekers of "wellness coaching" or manifestation-adjacent work
- Clients for whom English-language therapy isn't the right fit

Narrow positioning attracts the right clients *and repels the wrong ones*. Both matter.

## Target client (ICP)

### Primary: **The Quietly Burning Professional** (70% of marketing)

**Who she is:**
- 28–42, urban India (Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi, Pune, Bangalore NRIs)
- Manager to senior individual contributor, tech/finance/consulting/product
- Income: ₹25L–₹80L+/year (can afford ₹2500–₹5000/session without spreadsheet anxiety)
- Married or partnered, may have young kids or the weight of not-yet-kids
- English-fluent, Western-therapy-literate
- Has tried journaling, meditation apps, one ghosted therapist

**What she's actually feeling:**
- Competent at work, unravelling underneath
- Imposter syndrome *and* quiet superiority at the same time
- Lonely inside a life that looks good on Instagram
- Exhausted by managing everyone else's emotions
- A slow dread about the person they've become
- Grief they never named (miscarriage, parent's slow decline, a friendship that ended without words)

**What she believes about therapy:**
- Needed, probably for years
- Afraid it'll take forever
- Afraid the therapist won't "get" her world
- Afraid of being condescended to
- Wants clinical seriousness, not vibes

**What makes her book:**
- Website copy that names her life precisely
- A therapist whose CV reads like she's *been* in the room
- Clear boundaries (24h payment, cancellation tiers) — signals "this person takes the work seriously, so I can too"
- No pricing shock — handled via the "set in our first conversation" framing

### Secondary: **The Life-Transitions Seeker** (20%)

Marriage friction · postpartum identity loss · elder-care grief · relocation unmooring · partner mismatch in ambition · family system she's outgrown. Not in crisis, not high-functioning; in reckoning.

### Tertiary: **The Trauma-Informed Referral** (10%)

People specifically seeking IFS or a trauma-informed practitioner (often referred by another therapist, psychiatrist, or friend who did the work). Lower marketing priority — they arrive pre-convinced.

## Voice & tone

### Say it like this

> "Most weeks you're fine. Except for Sunday evenings. And the mornings you can't explain. And the way you've been short with your partner for reasons neither of you can name."

Specific. Observational. No diagnostic jargon until she earns it.

### Voice principles

| Principle | In practice |
|---|---|
| **Clinically precise** | Name modalities (IFS, CBT) and translate them — not "we'll work on your patterns" |
| **Corporate-literate** | She can say "performance review" without it feeling like work-from-a-script |
| **Restrained warmth** | Warm sentences, not warm *adjectives*. "I hear you" not "compassionate and healing" |
| **Structural honesty** | Tell them what therapy can and can't do. Name her limits. |
| **Spare punctuation** | Short sentences. Periods, not em-dashes. Generous whitespace. |
| **First-person direct** | "I work with…" "I don't do…" — she's on the page, not behind a curtain |

### Banned phrases (don't use any of these)

- "Safe space" (her current site uses it; it's now generic therapy-bingo)
- "Journey" (every therapist claims this; it means nothing)
- "Healing" as every-paragraph verb (overused; reserve for moments that earn it)
- "Empowerment" (overused; use a specific verb)
- "Authentic self" / "true self" (empty)
- "Transformation" (overpromise)
- "Trauma-informed therapy" as a standalone bullet — *show* it, don't announce it
- "Mental wellness" (corporate-HR flatlining)
- "Break the stigma" (cliche, also patronising for her ICP)
- "Tools and strategies" (on current site — vague and coach-y)
- Any phrase involving "energy," "vibrations," "alignment," "manifestation," "universe"
- "Navigate" (her current site uses this; replace with specific verbs)
- "Unpack" (therapy-Twitter tic)
- Emojis. Ever.

### Allowed-with-care

- "Inclusive" — but only if it pays off (e.g., a specific sentence like "I work with clients across gender identity and sexual orientation"). Don't leave it hanging.
- "Trauma" — use specifically, with IFS as the technical anchor
- "Evidence-based" — paired with the modality, not floating

## Story spine — the arc the site tells

### Before (the visitor's state)
You're functioning. You're performing. You're also exhausted in a way that doesn't go away on weekends. You've tried things. You're not sure therapy is for you — or rather, you're afraid it is.

### Turn (what she offers)
A practice that understands your world *and* the architecture underneath it. Clinical modalities (IFS, CBT) for the interior work. A therapist whose own résumé reads like yours until it didn't — then it branched into the thing you've been avoiding.

### After (the change)
Not a "transformed self." Something quieter. Clearer seeing. Less reactivity. Better sentences in the hard conversations. A less tangled relationship with your ambition, your family, your partner, your own earlier self.

## Core messages (the 7 things the site must land)

1. **You're not broken, and you're not the first person in a senior role to feel this way.**
2. **Therapy here is clinically serious work — evidence-based modalities, not a chat.**
3. **I understand the world you're trying to perform in — because I trained in it.** (PGDM moat)
4. **I work with the whole system — anxiety, trauma, relationships, family, workplace — because they aren't separate.**
5. **I hold firm boundaries (payment, cancellation, scope) because the work needs a container.**
6. **Online sessions, globally available, rooted in Bangalore.**
7. **The first 15 minutes are free. You'll know by the end of them whether we're a fit.**

## Tagline options (pick one for hero)

Ordered from most specific → most evocative:

1. **"Therapy for the ambitious life you're quietly exhausted by."**
2. **"Evidence-based therapy for high-performing people in unquiet seasons."**
3. **"For the inner life your résumé never lists."**
4. **"Clinical depth, without clinical distance."**
5. **"The therapist who reads the research and remembers the boardroom."** (most on-nose; maybe for the About section)
6. *(stretch)* **"You've been functioning. Let's work on living."**

My pick: **#1**. Names the ICP precisely. Carries the tension (ambition vs exhaustion) without being corporate-consulty. Implies the "PGDM + therapy" moat without announcing it.

## Key decisions made on hypothesis (for Dad & Pallavi to validate)

1. **Lead with the professional ICP.** Not anxiety-for-anyone.
2. **Surface PGDM.** Front-load, don't bury.
3. **Keep WhatsApp-first, add on-site intake form.** No live booking calendar.
4. **Reserve testimonial space, populate later.** Request 3–5 from Pallavi with client consent.
5. **Keep fees opaque, frame it strategically.** "Set in our first conversation based on your situation."
6. **Add location signal.** "Bangalore-based · online globally."
7. **Add a blog.** Low-volume (1–2/month) but builds SEO + depth.
8. **Fix session length to 60 min** (per T&C doc, authoritative source).
9. **Remove "Client Testimonials" heading until we can populate it.**
10. **Build in proper schema.org Person + MedicalBusiness markup** for SEO.

## What would change this whole document (open questions for Pallavi)

- Does she actually want to narrow to professionals, or does she deliberately serve broadly?
- How does *she* describe her "better client"?
- Is she comfortable foregrounding her PGDM? (Some therapists downplay management backgrounds to avoid being pigeonholed.)
- What has her IG voice been on? That reveals where she's naturally drawn.
- Does she do couples / family therapy in practice, or individual only?
- LGBTQ+ / queer-affirming — is that an active positioning or incidental? (Changes whether we lead with it or mention it.)
- Does she read in a specific Indian-language context, or is her practice English-only?
