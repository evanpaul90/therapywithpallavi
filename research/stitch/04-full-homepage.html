Who I work with

The clients who find me tend to arrive at a similar crossroads.

You have built a life that looks remarkable from the outside. The achievements are real, the trajectory is enviable, and you have become the person everyone relies on for stability and solutions.

But internally, the weight of maintaining this performance has become a silent tax. Perhaps you are experiencing a persistent restlessness that achievement no longer soothes, or a sudden fragility in relationships that once felt manageable.

I work with individuals who are accustomed to high levels of agency but find themselves facing internal landscapes—grief, burnout, relational patterns, or existential dread—that cannot be "optimized" away or solved with the usual strategies of discipline.

Our work together is about creating a space where you do not have to perform. It is about understanding the architecture of your inner life and finding a more sustainable, authentic way to inhabit the world you've built.

About

I work at the intersection of inner life and the world you perform in.

I am a psychotherapist focused on the unique psychological pressures of high-stakes environments. My background integrates traditional clinical training with a deep appreciation for the complexities of modern professional life.

I don't offer generic wellness advice. Instead, I provide a rigorous, evidence-based container for exploring the parts of yourself that have been sidelined in the service of productivity and success.

By blending Internal Family Systems (IFS) with Cognitive Behavioural frameworks, we address both immediate relief and long-term structural change in your psyche.

Areas of work

What we can work on together.

These aren't separate things. They pressurise each other.

The inner weather

Anxiety that feels like a constant hum, the specific exhaustion of burnout, and the persistent inner critic that fuels achievement but drains joy.

The relational work

Understanding attachment patterns, setting boundaries that actually hold, and navigating the isolation that often accompanies leadership.

What we carry quietly
